Research Proposal
Auditing AI Learning Assistants for Differential Service Quality
A Multi-Turn Correspondence Test Across Linguistic Registers
Core Question

Do AI learning assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) strengthen, maintain, or reduce service quality differences based on linguistic register as interaction progresses?

6,000
Sessions
30,000
AI Responses
3
Platforms

AI learning assistants may deliver systematically different service quality based on how a student writes — not what a student knows — and this differential treatment may compound over multi-turn interactions.

This proposal tests that claim with a large-scale correspondence audit adapted from Bertrand & Mullainathan (2004).

Research Gaps
Gap 1

Implicit Signals + Education

No study tests whether implicit linguistic register alone — without demographic labels — triggers differential educational service quality.

Gap 2

Multi-Turn Trajectory

Every empirical study is single-turn. Whether differential treatment intensifies or stabilizes over conversation is unknown.

Hypotheses
Seven predictions
H1
Existence. AI platforms provide different service quality across linguistic registers, with identical academic performance.
H2a
Trajectory — L1 Transfer. L1 transfer profiles exhibit flatter scaffolding trajectories than SAE profiles.
H2b
Trajectory — SAE. SAE profiles exhibit steeper trajectories — AI progressively offers more challenging content.
H2c
Path Dependence. Scaffolding gap is larger at Round 5 than Round 1.
H3
Recovery. Student self-correction at Round 3 does not shift L1 transfer trajectories. Linguistic first impression overrides competence evidence.
H4
Platform. Trajectory patterns differ across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.
H5
Strategy Shift. More directive, less Socratic instruction for L1 transfer; ratio gap widens over rounds.
H6
Gender. Female-named profiles receive different scaffolding than male-named within same register.
H7
Interaction. Gender bias is larger for L1 transfer profiles than SAE.
Method
Fully crossed correspondence test
5 registers × 2 gender × 2 performance × 3 platforms × 5 rounds × 20 reps = 6,000 sessions

Platforms: ChatGPT (GPT-4o), Claude (Sonnet), Gemini (1.5 Pro). Default temperature, no system prompt modifications.

Tasks: Introductory statistics, economics, academic writing — U.S. undergraduate level. Two performance levels (80% vs. 40%) crossed with all conditions.

Gender: Each register crossed with culturally appropriate gendered names, following Bertrand & Mullainathan (2004).

Power: 20 reps/cell → 100 observations/cell. >0.80 power at α = 0.05 for primary contrast.

Summary Metric

ASE Index = 1 − (max − min) / max

Algorithmic Service Equity — overall equity across conditions.
Analysis

Mixed-effects ANOVA with growth curve modeling — round as time variable, register as moderator.

Piecewise growth model tests H3's recovery hypothesis at Round 3 self-correction point.

Coding: 10% manually coded by two raters (Cohen's κ); remainder uses LLM-assisted coding with a model not in the study.

Implications
Anticipated outcomes
If H1–H2 Supported

First empirical evidence

Implicit register triggers differential educational quality. Multi-turn amplifies. Single-turn audits underestimate.

If H3 Supported

First impression fixation

Student competence cannot override linguistic profiling. Path dependence in algorithmic service.

If H1 Rejected

Boundary condition

Educational context is where dialect bias doesn't replicate — or multi-turn self-corrects.

If Mixed Platforms

Product differentiation

Some AI assistants perpetuate bias, others self-correct. Regulatory implications.

Why this matters
⚖️

Market Failure

Service quality determined by communication style, not need. Millions affected.

📋

Regulatory Signal

🔧
