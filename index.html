Research Proposal
Do AI tutors treat students differently based on how they write?
And does the gap get worse the longer the conversation goes?
Core Question

Do AI learning assistants strengthen, maintain, or reduce service quality differences based on linguistic register as interaction progresses?

Two gaps this study fills
Gap 1

Implicit signals in education

No study tests whether writing style alone — without naming race or nationality — triggers different tutoring quality.

Gap 2

Multi-turn trajectory

Every study so far is single-turn. We don't know if bias compounds or self-corrects over a conversation.

Predictions
Three things I expect to find
Prediction 1 — The gap exists

Same question, same knowledge, different help

Two students ask the exact same statistics question and demonstrate equal understanding. The only difference is writing style. The AI tutor gives the standard-English writer more challenging follow-ups, deeper explanations, and more Socratic questioning.
Test: H1 — Scaffolding quality differs across registers at identical performance levels.
Prediction 2 — It gets worse over time

Five rounds of conversation amplify the gap

By the 5th exchange, the standard-English writer is being pushed toward higher-order thinking. The L1-transfer writer is still getting basic confirmations. The gap at Round 5 is larger than at Round 1.
Test: H2 — Scaffolding trajectory slope is flatter for non-standard registers. H2c — Gap at Round 5 > Round 1.
Prediction 3 — Showing competence doesn't fix it

First impressions override demonstrated ability

At Round 3, the student gives a clearly correct, high-quality answer. If the student writes in standard English, the AI escalates. If the student writes with L1 transfer, the AI stays flat — treating the first impression as permanent.
Test: H3 — Self-correction at Round 3 does not shift the trajectory for non-standard registers.
Exploratory

Platform, strategy, and gender variation

H4–H7: Do ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini differ? Does the AI shift from Socratic to directive? Does name-based gender interact with register?
Method
Fully crossed correspondence test
5 registers × 2 gender × 2 performance × 3 platforms × 5 rounds × 20 reps = 6,000

Platforms: ChatGPT (GPT-4o), Claude (Sonnet), Gemini (1.5 Pro). Default settings, no system prompts.

Tasks: Intro statistics, economics, academic writing. Two performance levels (80% vs. 40%).

Summary Metric

ASE Index = 1 − (max − min) / max

Algorithmic Service Equity: 1.0 = identical treatment, 0.0 = maximum disparity.
Analysis

Mixed-effects ANOVA + growth curve modeling. Piecewise model tests whether the Round 3 competence signal actually shifts trajectory. 10% human-coded (Cohen's κ), remainder LLM-assisted.

Outcomes
What we learn either way
If confirmed

First empirical evidence

Writing style alone triggers differential tutoring. Multi-turn amplifies the gap.

If not confirmed

Boundary condition

Education is where dialect bias doesn't replicate — a meaningful finding.

If recovery fails

First impression lock-in

Competence can't override linguistic profiling once formed.

If platforms differ

Product differentiation

Some self-correct, some don't. Direct regulatory relevance.
