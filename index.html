Research Proposal
Auditing AI Learning Assistants for Differential Service Quality
Core Question

Do AI learning assistants strengthen, maintain, or reduce service quality differences based on linguistic register as interaction progresses?

6,000
Sessions
30,000
AI Responses

Platforms

AI tutors may deliver different quality based on how a student writes, not what they know — and this gap may compound over multi-turn interactions.

Research Gaps
Gap 1

Implicit Signals + Education

No study tests whether linguistic register alone triggers differential educational quality.

Gap 2

Multi-Turn Trajectory

Every study is single-turn. Whether bias compounds or self-corrects is unknown.

Hypotheses
Seven predictions
H1
Existence. Different service quality across registers, identical performance.
H2a
L1 Transfer trajectory. Flatter scaffolding slopes than SAE.
H2b
SAE trajectory. Steeper slopes — AI progressively challenges.
H2c
Path dependence. Gap larger at Round 5 than Round 1.
H3
Recovery failure. Self-correction at Round 3 does not shift L1 transfer trajectories.
H4
Platform variation. Patterns differ across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini.
H5
Strategy shift. More directive, less Socratic for L1 transfer; widens over rounds.
H6
Gender. Scaffolding differs by name gender within same register.
H7
Interaction. Gender bias larger for L1 transfer than SAE.
Method
Fully crossed correspondence test
5 registers × 2 gender × 2 performance × 3 platforms × 5 rounds × 20 reps = 6,000

Platforms: ChatGPT (GPT-4o), Claude (Sonnet), Gemini (1.5 Pro). Default temperature, no system prompts.

Tasks: Intro statistics, economics, academic writing. Two performance levels (80% vs. 40%).

Power: 20 reps/cell → >0.80 at α = 0.05 for primary contrast.

Summary Metric

ASE Index = 1 − (max − min) / max

Algorithmic Service Equity across conditions.
Analysis

Mixed-effects ANOVA + growth curve modeling. Piecewise model tests recovery at Round 3. 10% manual coding (Cohen's κ); remainder LLM-assisted with untested model.

Implications
Anticipated outcomes
If H1–H2 Supported

First empirical evidence

Implicit register triggers differential quality. Multi-turn amplifies.

If H3 Supported

First impression fixation

Competence cannot override linguistic profiling.

If H1 Rejected

Boundary condition

Education is where dialect bias doesn't replicate.

If Mixed Platforms

Product differentiation

Some platforms self-correct. Regulatory implications.
